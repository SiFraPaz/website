Section 1

Digital Resources & GIS Services

The resources below provide georeferenced data, WMS/WMTS services, and open vector datasets that can be loaded directly into QGIS, ArcGIS, or similar software. All are free of charge unless otherwise noted.

National Mapping Authority · Norway

Kartverket — Historical Map Archive

Norway's national mapping authority (Statens kartverk / Kartverket) holds approximately 10,000 historical land maps and 1,500 nautical charts dating back to the 1600s. Most are freely downloadable as scanned raster images. Key series include the Kvadratmilkart (c. 1774–1808), the Gradteigskart (printed from c. 1895), and the georeferenced Amtskart (county maps, 1826 onward, scale 1:200,000) — the most practical series for use as a GIS basemap.

WMS endpoint — load directly into QGIS / ArcGIS

https://wms.geonorge.no/skwms1/wms.historiskekart

Historical map archive & free download portal APIs and open data (Kartverket)

Higher-resolution GeoTIFF exports (beyond the free 300 dpi) are available on request for a fee. The historical map viewer is in Norwegian only.

National Geodata Portal · Norway

Geonorge

Norway's official open geodata portal, operated by Kartverket. Provides free downloads of topographic vector data at multiple scales (N50–N5000), current and historical administrative boundary files (kommuner, fylker), road networks, national elevation models, and place name datasets. Particularly useful for obtaining historical administrative boundary files at different dates (e.g., pre-2020 fylke boundaries), which are essential for historical GIS projects.

Geonorge — open geodata portal Map catalogue (search & download datasets) Historical maps dataset entry on data.norge.no

Online Map Viewer · Norway

Norgeskart

Kartverket's main public map viewer. Historical map layers — including the Amtskart series and the first edition of the Økonomisk kartverk — are accessible via the layer menu. Useful for previewing coverage before downloading or integrating via WMS. Updated weekly.

norgeskart.no

To access historical layers: open the map, click the layer icon, select "Bakgrunnskart" → "Historisk Amtskart".

Section 2

Digital Map Collections

These collections provide high-resolution scans of historical maps as primary sources. Maps typically require manual georeferencing before use as GIS basemaps, but they are invaluable for research into early modern and 19th-century cartographic representations of Norway.

National Library · Norway

Nasjonalbiblioteket (nb.no)

The National Library of Norway (Oslo) holds approximately 150,000 maps in total — the most extensive collection of printed maps of Norway, the Nordic countries, and the High North in existence. All maps printed in Norway since 1882 are held under the legal deposit scheme. The oldest items date to 1482. The Map Centre (Kartsenteret) is the library's dedicated map reading room and exhibition space, centred on the Ginsberg collection of c. 2,000 early maps, atlases, and geographical books. A large portion of the full collection has been digitised and is freely searchable and downloadable via the online library.

Map Centre overview (nb.no) Digital search portal — search kart + place name

Search tip: combine the Norwegian term kart with a place or region name to filter map results. Downloads are free. High-resolution orders can be requested directly from the library. Note that some items under copyright are accessible only from Norwegian IP addresses.

Private Collection · Stanford University

David Rumsey Map Collection

One of the world's largest online historical map collections, with over 150,000 items covering the 16th through 21st centuries. Norwegian and Scandinavian coverage is particularly strong for the early modern period. A notable feature is the built-in georeferencing tool, which overlays historical maps on modern geography directly in the browser. Many maps are also accessible as WMS/WMTS tiles for direct integration into QGIS.

Browse all Norway maps in the collection Viewers & WMS/WMTS access instructions

Free to access. The physical collection is housed at the David Rumsey Map Center, Stanford University Library.

Open Repository

Wikimedia Commons — Old Maps of Norway

A large collection of freely reusable historical map images, including many items digitised from the Nasjonalbiblioteket and other Norwegian institutions. Maps older than 70 years are in the public domain. Useful for quickly locating and downloading images for illustration or georeferencing.

Section 3

Norwegian Historical Atlases in Print

The Norwegian tradition of historical atlases in print is relatively modest. No comprehensive new research-grade atlas has been published since the 1980s. The works listed here are the principal reference tools; they are not directly usable as GIS basemaps but remain essential desk references.

Title Author / Editor Publisher Year Notes
Norsk historisk atlas Hagen, Rolf M. et al. J. W. Cappelens Forlag 1980 Research
The standard Norwegian historical atlas. 128 colour maps, covering c. 16,000 BC to AD 1975. Still the most comprehensive single-volume reference. Available via Norwegian university libraries and secondhand.
Norges historie, Bd. 15: Historisk atlas Mykland, Knut (ed.) Cappelen 1980 Research
The atlas volume of the 15-volume Norges historie series. Contains supplementary maps and indexes. Can be used independently.
Nasjonalatlas for Norge Various (thematic volumes) Statens kartverk 1979–2005 Thematic
A series of thematic volumes on topics including health (1996), sport (1997), vegetation (1998), climate, and population. Useful as geographic reference works, not as GIS basemaps. Available through Norwegian university libraries.
Historisk atlas — Cappelen Damm Various editions Educational
A pedagogically oriented world history atlas with Norwegian emphasis. Includes maps of the Norse medieval realm, Danish rule, and 1814–1905. Designed for upper secondary schools; not a research tool.

Suggested Workflow for QGIS

Historical basemap via WMS

Add the Kartverket WMS (wms.geonorge.no/skwms1/wms.historiskekart) as a raster layer. Select the Amtskart layer for 19th-century county-level coverage.

Vector boundaries from Geonorge

Download historical fylke and kommune boundary files from kartkatalog.geonorge.no. Multiple vintages are available (pre-2020, 2017, current). Use as editable overlay layers.

Georeference scanned maps

For earlier periods, download scans from the Nasjonalbiblioteket or Kartverket archive and georeference them manually in QGIS against the topographic base.

Wider Scandinavian context

Use the David Rumsey Collection for early modern maps with direct WMS integration, or the historical-basemaps GeoJSON dataset for continental-scale boundaries.
